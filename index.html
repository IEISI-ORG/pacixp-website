Bridging the Digital Divide

The Challenge

Currently, traffic routing among Pacific Island nations is highly inefficient. Neighbouring islands often reroute through Australia, Hawaii, or North America. This reliance on distant routing incurs high latencies (180-250ms) and significant transit costs, burdening ISPs and hampering regional digital transformation.

Crucially, this vulnerability affects critical communications during weather events. Rapid data transmission is vital for early warning systems and disaster response coordination across the Pacific.

The PACIXP Solution

PACIXP is establishing distributed, industry-standard Internet Exchange Points (IXPs) initially targeting Samoa and Fiji. By activating underutilised submarine cable capacity and providing direct peering between networks, we are eliminating unnecessary transit routes and fostering robust local internet ecosystems.

Our upcoming Fiji IXP deployment specifically aims to resolve domestic routing bottlenecks and provide a resilient hub for regional traffic exchange, supporting the Pacific Island Meteorological Strategy's goals for robust communication systems.

Key Benefits for Participants

For Internet Service Providers (ISPs)

  • Reduced Transit Costs: Achieve over 25% savings on transit costs, enabling more competitive pricing and improved service offerings.
  • Improved Performance: Provide faster and more reliable internet access, fostering customer satisfaction, loyalty, and growth.

For Content Providers

  • Enhanced User Experience: Lower latency translates to quicker loading times and seamless streaming.
  • Reduced Delivery Costs: Establish a direct presence in Pacific markets without the need for multiple independent deployments.
  • Direct Market Access: Effectively cater to local audiences and meet regional preferences with greater market penetration.

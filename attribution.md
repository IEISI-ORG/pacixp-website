# Projected Impact Methodology

The economic and performance targets presented here are grounded in comparable Internet Exchange Point deployments across the Asia-Pacific and Africa regions. The >35% traffic localization projection is consistent with early-stage IXP outcomes documented by the Internet Society (ISOC) in Kenya and Nigeria, where localization reached similar thresholds within the first years of operation, and reflects Fiji's existing ~41% local content caching baseline as measured by APNIC. The >25% ISP transit savings estimate draws on ISOC's IXP economic impact research, which found individual network savings of up to USD $240,000 annually even in lower-cost transit environments — a conservative benchmark given the significantly elevated wholesale transit pricing currently observed in Samoa and Fiji. The USD $1.2M–$1.8M annual regional savings projection is derived from applying these savings ratios to known wholesale transit costs in both markets, calibrated against African IXP case studies (KIXP, IXPN) scaled to Pacific market size. All figures represent early-stage projections and will be refined as PACIXP completes its feasibility study in partnership with APNIC and ISOC. Latency reduction targets are based on APNIC routing analysis of current Pacific inter-island paths and the measured performance improvements observed following the establishment of national IXPs in Fiji and Papua New Guinea.
